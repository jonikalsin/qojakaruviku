Potato Days festival to celebrate Idaho's agricultural heritage at Kleiner Memorial Park

July 2024 · 2 minute read

MERIDIAN, Idaho (CBS2) — Potato Days is coming to Meridian on Sept. 22-23, 2023, at Kleiner Memorial Park. The event is the largest celebration of the potato in Idaho and Idaho's rich agricultural heritage. Visitors can indulge in potato-based dishes, witness live performances, participate in engaging activities and learn facts about potatoes.

Dig into street food, games, art and music with your family. Potato Days boasts a massive installation featuring chalk art from over 60 local artists. There will also be a french fry competition, live performances in the band shell, carnival games, a vendor area, a bubble garden and bouncy houses.

Other highlights of Potato Days include:

  • Tantalizing Tater Treats: From baked potatoes to potato ice cream, sample an assortment of potato dishes prepared by local vendors.
  • Fry-Off: Watch as talented French fry chefs battle in a fry-off. Judges will select the top creations, and attendees can even get a taste of the competition dishes.
  • Potato-themed carnival games: Engage in family-friendly activities like potato sack races, potato crafts, free carnival games and a potato-themed photo booth. There will be entertainment for all ages throughout the event.
  • Potato Education: Learn about the cultivation process, nutritional benefits and the role of potatoes in Idaho's economy.
  • Live Music and Performances: Enjoy a lively atmosphere as you savor delicious food and dance to the beat.

Potato Days will occur at Kleiner Memorial Park behind The Village at Meridian at the corner of Eagle Road and Fairview. Admission to the event is free, with food and merchandise available for purchase from various vendors.
